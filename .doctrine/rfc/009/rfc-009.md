# RFC-009: Epistemic records as the human-facing relational substrate for design ambiguity

## Context

<!-- The forces at play: the problem, the question, and why it merits structured
     discussion. An RFC asserts NO canon — it is governance-neutral deliberation. -->

Doctrine's epistemic record taxonomy has four kinds — **assumption** (ASM — held
belief, unverified), **decision** (DEC — chosen path among alternatives), **question**
(QUE — unsettled matter requiring an answer), and **constraint** (CON — boundary that
must not be crossed). They are not quite all there. They feel like they should be able
to express more of the matter of software design — what was observed, what is being
tested, what a term *means* — while keeping edges hard enough that classification stays
reliable and no kind becomes a parallel implementation of another tool.

This RFC reframes an earlier draft. The earlier framing asked whether to add
**observation** and **hypothesis** to "complete an empirical loop" symmetric with the
normative cycle, motivated chiefly by actionability gating.

But **the gating motivation is spent.** RFC-008 resolved (→ ADR-017, accepted) to a
**kind-agnostic** gating model: an unsettled record gates via the trinary partition
and an inbound `needs` edge; there is no per-kind gating posture to design for. A new
kind participates in gating automatically through its status lifecycle. Design changes
should instead be driven by real usage needs.

The use-driven thesis: *epistemic records are the human-facing, lifecycle-bearing,
deeply relational data model for design ambiguity which dominates human work once
agents write and review code.* What is left when code authorship is cheap is
clarification, specification, review, and coordination — the confrontation of
ambiguity. That work wants a common language and structured tools shared by humans and
agents, not a private agent cache (memory) nor freeform prose. This RFC scopes that
schema, in two tiers (below), and is explicit that the near-term tier must stand on
its own even if the broader bet does not pay off.

### Two tiers

- **Tier 1 — near-term, standalone.** Improve and expand the modelling of the existing
  epistemic kinds and their edges, define the memory↔record boundary, and drive uptake
  by anchoring authoring/reading in skills. Justified independently of Tier 2.
- **Tier 2 — the broader hypothesis (experimental).** That a lightweight graph of typed,
  linked entities is a more useful medium than markdown for the most important work
  agents and developers do together — pushing specs from freeform text toward a graph of
  typed records, the way reviews are already a structured turn-taking artifact rather
  than prose. Compelling, unproven; Tier 1 is in its service but does not depend on it.

## Discussion

### Tier 1 — modelling the substrate

#### D1 — the memory↔record boundary

Memories and epistemic records both hold "knowledge," which is exactly why the boundary
must be hard. They differ on one axis: **ownership + legibility.**

- **Memory** is an *agent-owned private cache.* Auto-maintained, MCP-accessible,
  integrated search, staleness management, file globs, confidence/trust ratings — strong
  operational machinery, optimised for an agent retrieving what it learned so it works
  faster. It is human-opaque (or nearly), its `concept`/`fact`/`pattern` types are good
  *usage flags* but semantically blurry, and its links to real entities are
  second-class **by nature**: a memory points *at* the graph; it is not a node *in* it.
- **Epistemic record** is *shared, human-facing design state.* A real numbered node in
  `RELATION_RULES` with first-class edges in both directions, a lifecycle, and a posture
  meant to be read, edited, and argued with by humans and agents alike.

The classification test that keeps the edge hard and avoids parallel implementation:

> Need a real graph edge, human eyes, a lifecycle, or something to argue with? →
> **record.** A private note-to-self that helps an agent avoid re-learning? → **memory.**

Corollary: the "memory links are second-class" complaint is **not a memory bug to fix.**
Promoting memory links to first-class would rebuild records inside memories — the
parallel implementation we are trying to avoid. When first-class linkage is wanted, the
right move is to author a record. Whether the residual second-class-ness of memory links
is remediable or endemic is left open; it does not need to be resolved to hold this line.

A governing tension to name explicitly: you cannot simultaneously maximise *coverage*
("kinds express everything they feel like they should") and *crisp non-overlapping
edges* ("classification stays reliable"). This RFC chooses crisp edges and accepts that
records will not cover everything — the residual stays in prose and memory. The further
guard against ceremony: **records are optional crystallisation points that grow out of
prose, never a mandate to pre-structure ambiguity that is not ready.** Markdown's
looseness is a feature in the fuzzy phase. Reviews work as structured artifacts because
turn-taking is *inherently* structured; early design often is not yet.

#### D2 — which kinds we need

The corpus survey still stands as evidence of an expressive gap — re-read now as
"what design ambiguity has no typed home," not "what the empirical loop is missing."

A survey of 262 active doctrine memories (2026-06-26):

| memory type | count | knowledge shape |
|---|---|---|
| pattern | 178 | condensed observations, heuristics, footgun warnings |
| fact | 40 | empirical observation — "X is true, I observed it" |
| signpost | 23 | navigation — stays as memory |
| concept | 10 | explanation — "here's what X means / how it works" (only 2–3 are not shipped onboarding memories) |
| system | 8 | architectural truth |
| thread | 3 | ongoing exploration |

OBS and HYP are the **nearest-at-hand** candidates (carried from the earlier draft),
re-evaluated **as decomposition units of design ambiguity** — their value is graph
membership and lifecycle, *not* a bespoke gating posture. They are a starting pair, not
the answer to D2; the fuller question is what *the design-doc corpus itself* implies
(latent taxonomy, below).

**Observation (OBS)** — settled empirical truth, verified by direct experience.
Distinct from ASM (held-before-verified), DEC (no choice — a finding, not a decision),
CON (descriptive, not prescriptive). Lifecycle `observed → confirmed (terminal) /
retracted (terminal)`. Candidate facets: `finding`, `evidence`, `confidence`,
`supersedes`. Gating note: OBS is terminal-inert under ADR-017; its keep is earned via
relation-graph membership (a finding that *shapes* a slice, *supersedes* an earlier
observation), not via blocking. **Caution:** OBS is semantically broad ("I know a
thing") and epistemically narrow (settled/known only). That breadth fights D1's
crisp-edge bar — an unqualified OBS can swallow a risk, an invariant, an interaction, an
edge case. If OBS is adopted it likely wants *narrower siblings* (the latent taxonomy
below), not one catch-all empirical kind.

**Hypothesis (HYP)** — a testable proposed answer to a question. Distinct from QUE (the
unsettled matter itself) and ASM ("proceed as if true" vs "let's find out"). Lifecycle
`proposed → confirmed / refuted (terminal)`. Candidate facets: `proposition`,
`predicts`, `tested_by`, `confirmed_by`, `refuted_by`. Gating note: HYP(`proposed`) is
unsettled, so under ADR-017 it gates *for free* when a work item declares `needs →
HYP`; "spawn an investigation" is just `HYP spawns IMP`. No bespoke posture required —
the very mechanics the earlier draft treated as a reason to add HYP are now automatic.

**Coupling (OBS↔HYP).** If both are taken they are both-or-neither: HYP's terminal
"confirmed → becomes OBS" needs OBS as the landing state; OBS without HYP is a terminal
with no typed entry. (M1 rejected OBS-only; the symmetric argument rejects HYP-only.)
This is a constraint *within* the OBS/HYP pair — not the whole of D2.

**Risk does not fold away.** An earlier draft claimed risk-as-knowledge was a symptom of
the missing boundary, absorbed by OBS plus a downstream RSK. Retracted. Risk is a
first-class design and planning notion that features routinely in both; RSK-the-backlog-
item is too heavy to carry it inline; and OBS is the wrong tool — a risk is a *possible
future hazard*, not a settled finding (wrong tense, wrong epistemic status). Risk wants
its own lightweight typed home, or none — but it is not subsumed.

**The latent taxonomy (the real shape of D2).** Risk is one instance of a general point:
the unstructured design prose this RFC asserts we could improve *already encodes* a
richer type system than ASM/DEC/QUE/CON(/OBS/HYP). A pass over a sample of real design
docs is likely to surface, among others:

- **risk** — a possible future hazard
- **mitigation** — a planned response to a risk
- **invariant** — a property that must hold
- **principle / heuristic** — reusable guidance (what the 178 `pattern` memories crystallise)
- **procedure** — an ordered how-to
- **interaction** — a cross-component coupling (where review blockers concentrate)
- **responsibility** — an ownership / role assignment
- **edge case** — a boundary condition to handle
- **candidate solution** — an option under consideration (distinct from a chosen DEC)

This RFC adopts none of these now and dismisses none of them. The methodological
commitment of D2 is the inverse of reasoning from a memory-type table: **survey the
design-doc corpus to discover the latent taxonomy empirically**, then decide — kind by
kind, against D1's crisp-edge bar — which earn first-class typing, which stay prose, and
which are facets or edges rather than kinds. OBS/HYP are merely the two already on the
table; their adoption should wait on (or at least be checked against) that survey, lest a
broad OBS pre-empt the narrower kinds the prose actually wants.

A reified **concept/definition** is the other candidate, raised by D4 — kept there
because it has a different character from OBS/HYP and a naming collision to resolve.

#### D3 — first-class edges among records and entities (the bulk of the modelling work)

If records are the substrate, the relation vocabulary among them is where most of the
near-term value lives. Today the four kinds author `shapes`, `spawns`, `governed_by`,
and `supersedes`; new kinds inherit these via the `RECORD` grouping (no `RELATION_RULES`
changes for existing labels — confirmed by the edge-validity snapshot in
`edge-validation.md`). The work is to decide what *more* the substrate needs:

- typed lifecycle edges for the empirical chain (`confirmed_by` / `refuted_by` HYP→OBS)
  — modelled as `LifecycleOnly` (authored through a transition verb, like `supersedes`),
  not `link`-writable;
- whether `shapes` wants the record→record (epistemic) vs record→work (affects)
  disambiguation that ADR-016 decoupled and RFC-003 reopens;
- the `describes` / `depends on` / `decomposes` relationship types the concept maps
  express but records cannot (CM-001, CM-002) — which of these are real substrate edges
  vs projections a consumer should derive (RFC-003's *derivable, not relational* law).

This is deliberately the largest Tier-1 surface and the one most likely to deliver
near-term utility independent of Tier 2.

#### D4 — concept maps: retire-as-is vs reify

Concept maps today are an entity wrapping a DSL whose **virtual nodes are orphaned from
the rest of the graph** — strings inside a container, not entities. This is the same
disease as memory's second-class links and the `concept`-memory/CM overlap: things that
should be graph nodes are trapped inside a container. One cure — **reify.** A
concept/definition becomes a first-class numbered entity with ontological edges to other
entities; a concept map becomes a **view/projection over real concept-nodes** rather
than a private DSL. That simultaneously dissolves the orphan problem, the
`concept`-memory overlap (only 2–3 such memories exist that are not shipped onboarding
docs), and the CM/record overlap.

Naming collision to resolve: **CON is taken (constraint).** A reified concept needs its
own prefix (e.g. DEF/CPT); do not overload CON.

This decision is the modest one — lower upside than the spec-as-graph bet, lower chance
of being completely wrong. It may also be that concept maps *in their current form* have
no clear place in doctrine proper, and that the value is entirely in reified
concepts + ontological associations, with the DSL retired.

#### D5 — uptake via skills

The real failure mode is not modelling but adoption: a kind nobody authors is a dead
letter (cf. the 2–3 lonely `concept` memories). Whatever Tier-1 kinds and edges land
must be anchored in skills — authoring and reading paths routed the way other work is —
or they will not be used regardless of how clean the model is. IMP-182 (the `/knowledge`
authoring skill) is the seed; this decision asks how far skill-anchoring must go for the
substrate to actually take hold.

### Tier 2 — the broader hypothesis (experimental)

A blurry but compelling vision: epistemic records model the ambiguity inherent in
software design. Estimation is anchored in the human effort of clarification, review,
clear specification, and coordination with others — what remains when agents can write
and review code. That ambiguity wants to be confronted by users and agents through a
*common language and a shared set of structured tools.*

The concrete experimental move: **specs become less freeform text and more a graph of
typed entities** — open questions, decisions, assumptions, observations, connected and
linked — analogous to how reviews are highly structured artifacts for turn-taking
exchanges. This has a different character and different operational characteristics from
memories: lighter, with unique lifecycles, human-facing, and deeply relational with other
entities. A lightweight graph — *if references and tools are low-friction enough* — could
be a more useful medium than markdown for much of the most important work agents and
developers do together.

This is unproven and explicitly a hypothesis, not a plan. Its chief risk is **ossifying
into ceremony**: a typed graph imposed on ambiguity that is not ready hardens too early
and loses the very looseness that prose gives the fuzzy phase (see D1). The mitigation is
that Tier 1 is justified standalone, and that records crystallise out of prose rather
than replacing it. If Tier 2 proves out, this RFC may **poop a child** RFC that carries
the spec-as-graph experiment concretely; for now it stays a labelled bet, with Tier 1
the thing actually advanced.

### Alternatives considered

**M0 — status quo.** Keep ASM/DEC/QUE/CON, all knowledge-shaped residual in memories.
Tenable, and the honest default if D2 lands "neither": the corpus gap can be carried by
prose + memory. Rejected as the *whole* answer only because it leaves the substrate
vision (Tier 2) unexplored and the boundary (D1) undefined.

**M-memory — fix it on the memory side.** Promote memory types to first-class kinds and
memory links to graph edges. Rejected: this rebuilds records inside memories — the
parallel implementation D1 exists to prevent — and does not give humans a legible,
arguable artifact.

**M-fold — subsume the new kinds into existing ones.** HYP into QUE (a question with a
proposed-answer field) or OBS into ASM (`confidence=high`, `basis=observation`).
Rejected as before: HYP demands investigation, not deliberation; OBS is a settled
terminal an assumption cannot represent. But note these are now *taxonomy* arguments, not
gating ones.

## Outcome

<!-- If resolved: what was concluded. If open: what remains unanswered. -->

**Open — reframed deliberation seed.** The RFC is recast from "complete the empirical
loop" (gating-motivated, symmetry-driven) to "epistemic records as the human-facing
relational substrate for design ambiguity," in two fenced tiers. Decisions to converge:

- **D1 — memory↔record boundary.** *Settled in this draft:* ownership/legibility test;
  records are graph-resident and human-facing, memories are agent-owned cache; crisp
  edges over coverage; records crystallise out of prose. Load-bearing — everything else
  rests on it. (Remaining open thread: is memory's second-class linkage endemic?)
- **D2 — which kinds.** Survey the design-doc corpus for the latent taxonomy (risk,
  mitigation, invariant, principle, procedure, interaction, responsibility, edge case,
  candidate solution, …); decide kind by kind against D1's crisp-edge bar what earns
  typing vs stays prose vs is a facet/edge. OBS/HYP are the nearest-at-hand pair
  (both-or-neither if taken), not the whole answer; risk is **not** subsumed. The
  reified-concept candidate routes through D4.
- **D3 — record/entity edges.** The bulk of near-term work: typed lifecycle edges
  (`confirmed_by`/`refuted_by`), the `shapes` epistemic-vs-affects split, and which
  concept-map relationship types are real substrate edges vs derivable projections.
- **D4 — concept maps.** Retire-as-is vs reify-as-view-over-concept-nodes; resolve the
  CON naming collision for a reified concept kind. The modest, lower-risk decision.
- **D5 — uptake.** How far skill-anchoring must go for the substrate to take hold.
- **Tier 2 — the hypothesis.** Stated explicitly; not for convergence here. May spawn a
  child RFC for the spec-as-graph experiment.

Convergence of D2/D3/D4 that adds kinds, edges, or a reified concept emits a Revision
(ADR-013) against the kind catalog / relation model / entity engine.
