# RFC-009: Epistemic record kinds: observation, hypothesis, and the empirical loop

## Context

<!-- The forces at play: the problem, the question, and why it merits structured
     discussion. An RFC asserts NO canon — it is governance-neutral deliberation. -->

Doctrine's epistemic record taxonomy has four kinds: **assumption** (ASM — held belief,
unverified), **decision** (DEC — chosen path among alternatives), **question** (QUE —
unsettled matter requiring an answer), and **constraint** (CON — boundary that must not
be crossed). These form a partial normative cycle (QUE → DEC → CON, underpinned by
ASM), but they have no empirical counterpart — no way to record *what was observed* or
*what might be true and warrants testing*.

This matters for RFC-008 (actionability gating): the gating mechanism gates *records of
specific kinds*, and what kinds exist determines what `shapes` edges mean and what
gating dynamics are possible. A survey of the 262 active doctrine memories and two
concept maps (CM-001, CM-002) surfaced gaps in the taxonomy that bear directly on the
gating design.

### The question

> Should doctrine add **observation** (OBS — settled empirical truth) and **hypothesis**
> (HYP — testable proposed answer) to the epistemic record taxonomy, completing an
> empirical loop that complements the existing normative cycle?

## Discussion

### Evidence: memory corpus survey

A survey of 262 active doctrine memories (2026-06-26) reveals the knowledge shapes
currently captured only as memories, with no epistemic-record outlet:

| memory type | count | knowledge shape |
|---|---|---|
| pattern | 178 | condensed observations, heuristics, footgun warnings |
| fact | 40 | empirical observation — "X is true, I observed it" |
| signpost | 23 | navigation — stays as memory |
| concept | 10 | explanation — "here's how X works" |
| system | 8 | architectural truth |
| thread | 3 | ongoing exploration |

**Facts** are the clearest gap. Examples:

- "clap ColorChoice parser is case-sensitive"
- "Cordage Graph has no public edge_count"
- "Runtime CARGO_MANIFEST_DIR beats env! under shared target"

These are empirical, verified-by-experience, and settled — not assumptions
(held-before-verified), not decisions (no choice), not constraints (descriptive, not
prescriptive). They currently have no epistemic-record home.

**Patterns** are a mixed bag. Many are condensed observations crystallized as
heuristics ("review the seams, not just the components" — earned through four review
rounds where every blocker was an interaction bug). Others are hazard observations
("expect(dead_code) on `mod foo;` propagates into the submodule") that read more
like risks than principles. The 178 patterns suggest a missing terminal state for the
empirical chain: observations that have been condensed into reusable guidance.

### Evidence: concept map relationship gaps

CM-001 (Requirements) and CM-002 (Workflow) express relationship types with no
epistemic-record outlet:

- **`depends on`** — records can't express dependencies on each other or on work items
  (`needs`/`after` is slice/backlog-only; `shapes` is associative, not dependency)
- **`describes`** — definitional, stronger than `shapes` ("this spec describes that
  domain" is not merely "this spec shapes that domain")
- **`belongs to` / `decomposes`** — structural hierarchy (RFC-003 D axis, unbuilt)
- **`is gated by`** — RFC-008's subject, doesn't exist yet

A richer kind taxonomy may need richer relation vocabulary. Observations and
hypotheses, in particular, relate to other entities differently than assumptions and
questions do.

### Candidate kinds

#### Observation (OBS)

Settled empirical truth, verified by direct experience.

| axis | shape |
|---|---|
| **question answered** | "what did we find?" |
| **posture** | settled, evidence-backed |
| **distinction from ASM** | ASM is held-before-verified; OBS is verified-by-experience |
| **distinction from DEC** | no choice involved — it's a finding, not a decision |
| **distinction from CON** | descriptive, not prescriptive |
| **gating posture** | terminal — a confirmed observation is inert (like a settled question). An observation of a *problem* ("X is broken") could gate work until understood, but the observation itself is settled fact. |
| **lifecycle** | `observed` → `confirmed` (terminal) / `retracted` (terminal) |
| **resolution** | confirmed by evidence; retracted if disproven |

Example facet fields: `finding` (what was observed), `evidence` (how it was observed
— direct inspection, experiment, reproduction steps), `confidence` (low/medium/high),
`supersedes` (this observation supersedes an earlier one).

#### Hypothesis (HYP)

A testable proposed answer to a question.

| axis | shape |
|---|---|
| **question answered** | "what might be true?" |
| **posture** | proposed, needs testing |
| **distinction from QUE** | QUE is the unsettled matter; HYP is a candidate resolution |
| **distinction from ASM** | ASM says "proceed as if true"; HYP says "let's find out" |
| **gating posture** | may gate OR spawn — a hypothesis might block work pending investigation, or it might *spawn* investigation work (an IMP/ISS to test it). Different from ASM's uniform gate posture. |
| **lifecycle** | `proposed` → `confirmed` (becomes OBS) / `refuted` (terminal) |
| **resolution** | confirmed by observation(s); refuted by contrary evidence |

Example facet fields: `proposition` (the testable claim), `predicts` (what would be
true if correct), `tested_by` (how it was/will be tested), `confirmed_by` (the OBS
that confirmed it), `refuted_by` (the evidence that refuted it).

### The empirical loop

The current four kinds form a partial normative cycle. The proposed two complete an
empirical cycle:

```
                   normative                         empirical
              ┌──────────────────┐            ┌──────────────────────┐
              │                  │            │                      │
              ▼                  │            ▼                      │
QUE ──proposes answer──▶ HYP ──confirmed by──▶ OBS ──crystallizes──▶ heuristic
 │                         │                     │
 └── underpinned by ────── ASM                   │
              │                                  │
              ▼                                  │
             DEC ──gated by──────────────────── CON
```

- **HYP** is the bridge between unsettled (QUE) and settled (OBS)
- **OBS** is the terminal empirical state — confirmed, evidence-backed
- **Heuristics** (what memories call "patterns") are the end state of the empirical
  chain — condensed observations, not a separate kind
- The current taxonomy has no empirical terminal; everything is either unsettled
  (QUE), provisional (ASM), prescriptive (DEC, CON), or chosen (DEC)

### Risk vs knowledge tension

Many of the 178 pattern memories are really observations of hazards:
"expect(dead_code) propagates into the submodule." Today risks are backlog items
(RSK) with priority/estimate/blocking semantics but no evidentiary chain. An OBS
could carry the evidence ("we observed this footgun under these conditions"); a
downstream RSK could reference it.

Open question: should risks also exist as epistemic records — a lighter-weight
analog of RSK, capturing "this is dangerous" as *knowledge* rather than *work*?
Not resolved here, but surfaced by the corpus survey.

### Bearing on RFC-008

The gating mechanism is directly affected by the kind taxonomy:

1. **Gating dynamics differ by kind.** QUE gates (unknown answer). ASM gates
   (unverified premise). HYP might gate-or-spawn (investigation, not deliberation).
   OBS is terminal-inert (settled fact). The gating model should not treat all
   unsettled records uniformly — posture matters.

2. **`shapes` edge semantics vary by kind.** HYP→QUE ("this hypothesis proposes an
   answer to that question") is different from ASM→QUE ("this assumption underpins
   that question") and different from OBS→SL ("this observation bears on that
   slice"). The intra-record `shapes` question (RFC-008 D-f, RFC-003) is amplified
   by a richer kind taxonomy.

3. **Sequencing.** If OBS and HYP are adopted, the gating mechanism should be
   designed for six kinds, not four — retrofitting gating semantics for two new
   kinds after the mechanism lands is costlier than designing for them now.

### Alternatives considered

**M0 — status quo.** Keep ASM/DEC/QUE/CON. Rejected: the memory corpus shows a real
gap (40 facts, 178 patterns with no epistemic outlet), and the gating design needs
clarity on what it's gating.

**M1 — OBS only.** Add observation, defer hypothesis. Partially rejected: OBS alone
creates a terminal without a bridge — you can record what you found, but not what
you're testing. HYP is the entry point to the empirical loop.

**M2 — fold HYP into QUE.** A question with a proposed-answer field subsumes
hypothesis. Rejected: a hypothesis is qualitatively different — it demands
investigation, not deliberation. QUE says "we don't know"; HYP says "we think we
know, let's test." The gating posture differs.

**M3 — fold OBS into ASM.** An assumption with `confidence=high` and
`basis=observation` subsumes observation. Rejected: an assumption is held-for-now
and gates work; an observation is settled and (usually) inert. Conflating them
loses the empirical terminal.

## Outcome

<!-- If resolved: what was concluded. If open: what remains unanswered. -->

**Open — deliberation seed.** Two candidate kinds (OBS, HYP) are proposed with
definitions, facet fields, status lifecycles, and gating postures. The empirical
loop is modelled. Decisions to converge:

- **D-a — adopt OBS?** Does the taxonomy need a settled-empirical terminal? Evidence:
  40 fact memories with no epistemic outlet; gating needs a terminal-inert posture.
- **D-b — adopt HYP?** Does the taxonomy need a testable-proposition bridge between
  QUE and OBS? Evidence: the empirical loop has no entry point without it; HYP's
  gate-or-spawn posture is distinct from QUE and ASM.
- **D-c — risk-as-knowledge?** Should risks exist as epistemic records (lighter-weight
  RSK analog) in addition to backlog items? Evidence: 178 pattern memories, many of
  which are hazard observations.
- **D-d — relation vocabulary for new kinds.** If OBS and HYP are adopted, what
  `shapes` semantics do they carry? Does the intra-record edge vocabulary need
  expansion (RFC-003, RFC-008 D-f)?
- **D-e — sequencing with RFC-008.** If adopted, does the kind taxonomy land before
  or after the gating mechanism? Leaning: taxonomy first — design gating for the
  full set.

If D-a/D-b converge, emit a Revision adding OBS and HYP to the kind catalog
(KINDS, entity engine, `knowledge new`, statuses, templates).
