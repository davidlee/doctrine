## Finish Axis B — retire `slices` / `drift` (folded 2026-06-29)

SL-149 collapsed the work→**canon** noun-labels (`specs`, `requirements`) into the
universal grammar. It left the work→**backlog** noun-labels standing — **`slices`** and
**`drift`**. They are the *unfinished half of Axis B*: labels named after their target
kind, not the verb, at a coordinate SL-149 did not reach. `slices` conflates **three**
dimensions in one binary edge:

- **provenance** — which entity was born from which (IMP-207's 19 `doctor`-flagged rows,
  including IMP-095, are this dimension mislabelled as fulfillment);
- **fulfillment** — this slice does work toward the item;
- **completion** — *how much* of the item the slice satisfies.

RFC-003 under-saw this: it dispositioned `slices` as merely *temporal* (IMP-149, "derive
planned/done from slice status"). That derivation **cannot express partial** — a closed
slice that only partially satisfies an item must not read as "done." Completion is a fact
about the (slice, item) *pair*, derivable from neither endpoint — the one genuine exception
to the *derivable, not relational* law (§ Layer 2), and exactly the `partially_addresses`
predicate the law rejected. The rejection assumed a coverage substrate `slices` never had.

### The resolution — everything onto the one grammar

| dimension | edge | notes |
|---|---|---|
| provenance | **`references(originates_from)`** | one universal role: *"I was born from the target."* Any work entity → its origin. Doesn't gate. |
| fulfillment + completion | **`fulfils`** label, **`{full, partial}` degree facet** | slice → backlog. The *only* gating edge (close-cascade). Payload-bearing. |
| addressed-by view | *(derived)* | the old `slices` reading is the **inbound reciprocal** of `fulfils` (ADR-004), not a stored label. |

**Decisions locked (2026-06-29):**

- **One neutral `originates_from` role** — not sub-roles. It subsumes the shipped
  `references(scoped_from)` (SL→backlog), absorbs IMP-207's proposed `spawned_from`
  (backlog→SL), and covers SL→SL splits and `drift`'s "carved out from" row. Sub-roles
  (`scoped` deliberate vs `follow_up` emergent) are deferred until an edge demands the
  distinction.
- **Degree is a facet — not a role, not a substrate.** `{full, partial}` rides the
  `fulfils` edge (Route 1). The heavier coverage substrate (sub-acceptance on backlog
  items, à la SPEC-002 reconciliation) is deferred until backlog items gain sub-units.

### What retires

| today | becomes |
|---|---|
| `slices` (BACKLOG→SL, noun, conflated) | **retired** → derived inbound of `fulfils` |
| `references(scoped_from)` | generalized → `references(originates_from)` |
| `spawned_from` (IMP-207, proposed) | never minted; absorbed into `originates_from` |
| `drift` entity rows ("carved out from" / "feeds into") | `originates_from` / dep-seq layer |
| completion hole (Axis C, rejected `partially_addresses`) | `fulfils` degree facet |
| `drift` non-entity rows (memory / file) | still deferred → non-entity edge |

Migration is **SL-149 redux** — the role-backfill + label-retirement machinery exists.
`drift` retires fully only once the deferred non-entity edge ships; its entity rows fold now.

### Constraints carried into the slice

- **Author-at-the-mutable-end.** `originates_from` and `fulfils` are both authored at the
  *live* end (the born entity; the closing slice) — not a fixed source kind. This is a new
  modelled property: the authoring end is dictated by lifecycle state. ADR-004 (outbound-
  only) still holds; this refines *which* end is outbound.
- **Completion is a hint, never auto-close (F-6).** A `fulfils(full)` makes the item a
  *candidate* for closure; the cascade is a `doctor` / `/close` hint a human confirms.
  `{full, partial}` does not aggregate (two partials ≠ full), so item-completion over a set
  of inbound `fulfils` edges is a judgement, not arithmetic — by design, unless the
  substrate (Route 2) ever lands.
- **Each slice authors its own `fulfils` at close** — partial→full is a *new* slice's new
  edge, never an edit to a closed slice's edge.

### Disposition

A decided direction, not an open question. Needs a **ratifying ADR** (amend ADR-016 /
ADR-010, or a sibling) and **one implementation slice** (finish-Axis-B). It discharges
**IMP-207** (the 19-row retcon folds in — they become `originates_from`), **IMP-149**
(`slices` ambiguity dissolved), and **Axis C's completion hole** in a single coherent move.

## Context

Doctrine's entity-relationship model grew incrementally — SL-046 (reader), SL-048
(writer + tier-1 migration), SL-095 (governance supersession), SL-136 (tags), and
ad-hoc extensions — governed by ADR-004 (outbound-only) and ADR-010 (tier partition +
contract unification), with SPEC-018 capturing the cross-corpus contract. It has never
been reviewed **holistically**. CHR-024 drives that review; this RFC is its
deliberation deliverable.

The live vocabulary is **20 outbound labels** in one const table
(`RELATION_RULES`, `src/relation.rs`), each declaring `(sources, label) → target ·
tier · link-policy · inbound_name`. The model lavished structure on **lineage**
(`descends_from`/`parent`/`members`/`interactions`, four tier-2 labels) and on
**knowledge records** (`shapes`/`spawns`, reaching 16 kinds), while the busiest
real-world traffic — **work → artefact** — got exactly one overloaded label per
direction (`specs`, `slices`). That asymmetry is the problem.

### The four axes (CHR-024 findings, triaged)

The seven preliminary findings (F-1..F-7) plus the friction backlog items collapse to
four axes at different altitudes:

| axis | problem | disposition |
|---|---|---|
| **A** backlog second-class | `governed_by`/`related`/`reviews` exclude BACKLOG sources (F-1/F-2/F-4/F-5) | **easy win — slice now**, source-set extension; need not wait on this RFC |
| **B** overloaded edge intent | `specs`/`slices`/`related` conflate distinct intents (F-3, IMP-149) | **this RFC's purpose** |
| **C** coverage / completion | no partial-satisfaction, premature-close risk (F-6, IMP-141) | **not a vocabulary problem** → `validate`/`/close`/SPEC-002 |
| **D** decomposition / hierarchy | no slice-split, no epic, no altitude lattice (F-7) | **carve out as a sibling feature/spec**; its modelling informs B |

### The question

> Should doctrine separate **durable structural relation shape** from **contextual
> role-intent** — keeping a small primitive vocabulary refined by a closed role
> grammar — while leaving actionability, priority, coverage, and closure to their
> owning consumers?

## Discussion

### Empirical grounding (P1 classification)

Before theorising, the B-relevant edge population was classified exhaustively from
`doctrine relation list` (authoritative, all `resolved`). **Snapshot — P1 census, 2026-06-23:**
939 edges total; B-relevant = `specs` (60) + `related` (48) + `drift` (5) + `slices`
(77, temporal); classified population = **113** (`specs`+`related`+`drift`).

> **Snapshot caveat.** The corpus grows; later censuses drift. As of a re-run while
> reviewing this RFC the live counts were ~951 total, `related` **60** (the classified
> figure above was 48), so the `specs`+`related`+`drift` population is ~125, not 113.
> The ~12 newer `related` edges are **unclassified** — the completeness verdict below is
> "role-complete **as of the snapshot**," not a standing guarantee. Re-classify before
> treating these counts as live proof.

Classified against a candidate role grammar `{implements, reviews, scoped_from,
bears_on, related}`:

- **The grammar is complete for entity→entity edges** *as classified at the snapshot*.
  It covers **100%** of the 113 `specs`/`related`/`drift` edges then resolved. No edge
  demands a role outside the set. (Caveat: 3 of
  the 5 `drift` edges target memory/file — the role is assignable but the *target* is
  not expressible until the non-entity edge ships; see the boundary section. "Complete"
  here means role-complete, not target-complete.)
- **The `reviews` *role* has zero instances in the reference family** — and must not be
  confused with the live first-class **`reviews` label** (`RV → AnyNumbered`,
  `Tier::Typed`, `TypedVerbOnly`, ~145 edges in `RELATION_RULES`), which is the
  structured RV review-subject edge and is **NOT** collapsed into `references`. The
  reference-family `reviews` role is empty precisely *because* reviewing is already a
  first-class typed edge carried by RV records; the role is reserved for a *non-RV*
  reviewer outlet (BACKLOG/SL → target, Axis A) and is otherwise redundant with the RV
  typed edge. Absent-edge, not refuted.
- **The current labels mismap intent at scale** — the grammar *corrects*, not merely
  renames:
  - `specs`: `SL→` = implements (~44, fine); **`IMP→`/`RSK→` mean bears_on/scoped_from**
    (12 mismapped — a risk cannot *implement* a spec; an improvement's spawned slice
    implements, the improvement itself only bears on).
  - `related`: the peer reading is the **minority** — `RFC→bag` = bears_on (~26),
    `SL→backlog` = **scoped_from** (~13), `GOV→GOV` = pure related (4), `SL→SL` =
    peer/seq (4).
  - `drift` (5): a free-text escape hatch holding bears_on (×3), decomposition (×1,
    "carved out from" = F-7 in the wild), and sequence (×1).
- **`exclusive_with`: zero instances** — genuinely speculative.
- **`slices`: 100% BACKLOG→SL, temporal** — see the design law below.

~25 of ~113 edges assert an intent their label gets wrong. This is not cosmetic.

### Layer 1 — structure is not graph-effect

Whether an edge *gates work*, *evicts an alternative*, or *scores consequence* is a
**consumer decision**, not a property of the relation. `A part_of B` is structurally
true regardless of whether the priority engine cares. So:

- **Relation contract** (`RELATION_RULES`): structural truth — sources, target, tier,
  role grammar. Durable.
- **Consumer policy** (priority overlay / `/close` / `validate`): graph-effect, gating,
  eviction, closure. A *projection* over selected edges.

A label's gating behaviour is **declared in the consumer**, not the vocabulary —
IMP-047 (trinary actionability) is the principled version of that assignment (today a
new label silently gets `Reject,Unbounded` until the overlay assigns policy).
Actionability (`needs`/`after`/IMP-047 `gates`) belongs to the dep/seq layer, not the
semantic relation model.

### Layer 2 — the design law: *derivable, not relational*

> **Do not encode in the relation what is derivable from entity state or facets.** The
> edge carries durable structural intent; status, coverage, and altitude *project* over
> it.

This law surfaced three times — three "apparent missing labels" that are really
projections:

- **Coverage (C)** — not a label → `validate` / `/close` / SPEC-002. Corollary:
  `partially_addresses` is a **C-predicate, explicitly kept OUT of the role grammar** —
  partial satisfaction is a coverage projection, not a structural intent.
- **Temporal (`slices` planned-vs-done, IMP-149)** — not a label → derive from the
  target slice's **lifecycle status**. Minting `planned_by`/`completed_by` is a category
  error: it forces *rewriting the edge as status moves*. The edge is stable; the status
  moves. This dissolves the structural half of IMP-149 at **zero** vocabulary cost.
  *Caveat:* "open = planned, closed = done" is only sound if the lifecycle→reading map is
  normative — closure-without-delivery states (cancelled / superseded / abandoned) must
  map to their own reading, not silently to "done." The projection is deferred to
  lifecycle semantics; the claim is "no new label," not "the mapping is trivial."
- **Altitude (SL→PRD vs SL→SPEC)** — not a role → derive from the target's altitude
  **facet** (see Decomposition below).

### The deciding principle — label vs role

> **Separate LABEL** when the distinction changes the edge's **structure, graph-effect,
> or inbound semantics.**
> **ROLE facet** when it only refines the **intent** of an otherwise
> structurally-identical edge.

Applied:

| candidate | differs in… | verdict |
|---|---|---|
| implements / reviews / scoped_from / bears_on | intent only (same structure: work→canon, consequence-scored, no gate) | **roles** on `references` |
| `part_of` | structure: hierarchy, tree render, close-gate | **label** |
| `supersedes` | lifecycle + reverse carve-out | **label** |
| `exclusive_with` | symmetric + (future) evict | **label** |
| `governed_by` | authority semantics + distinct "governs" inbound | **label** (stays) |
| `related` | weakest peer — the degenerate no-intent reference | **collapses** → `references(role=related)` |

### Type safety survives the collapse

The objection to a generic `references` label is that it sacrifices the target-kind
gate (`specs → {PRD,SPEC}`) that the validator enforces today. It does not — the gate
**relocates from the label to the role**:

- Move target validation from `(source, label) → TargetSpec` to
  `(source, label, role) → TargetSpec`.
- **Two-level closed grammar:**
  - `(source_kind, label) → legal roles` — e.g. `references` from SL admits
    `{implements, reviews, scoped_from, bears_on}`; from RV admits `{reviews}` only.
  - `(source_kind, label, role) → TargetSpec` — e.g. `references(implements) →
    {SPEC,PRD,REQ}`; `references(reviews) → AnyNumbered`. (Target sets are
    corpus-proven: `specs→{PRD,SPEC}` and the separate `requirements→{REQ}` label fold
    in here. **ADR is deliberately excluded** — see the open question below; ADR
    authority stays on `governed_by`.)
- **Roles are a closed enum**, not free text.

The *rule table* is not new machinery: `RELATION_RULES` is *already* multi-row per label
(`related` ×2, `supersedes` ×3); it gains one column, and the lockstep enum-order test
and overlay allocation survive unchanged. (The *seam* beyond the table — storage row,
`lookup`, `validate_link`, surfaces — is real work; see Costs #3.)

`specs`'s real defect was a **noun verbing** — the missing verb *is* the role. Once the
role supplies the verb, the target set widens to actual canon and becomes
role-determined. "These 3 REQs" = three `references(implements)→REQ`; "this entire PRD"
= `references(implements)→PRD`.

A property falls out for free: because the role names the verb, the **inbound reciprocal
is role-derived** — `inspect SPEC-018` reads "implemented by SL-x · reviewed by CHR-024
· scoped SL-y" instead of a flat "specs ×3". (Whether to take this vs label-flat inbound
is the one sub-decision left open.)

### The boundary the role grammar cannot absorb

P1 found `drift` pointing at **memory entities (×2) and a file path (×1)** — targets
*outside the numbered-entity space*. The `references`+role grammar is **entity→entity**;
it does not cover backlog→memory or backlog→file. `drift` is abused precisely because no
structured outlet exists. A future edge shape is needed:

```
(label + role)[src: entity] → [target: non-entity | file | glob | vec-of-files]
```

This is the home for **IMP-012** (architectural triggers — structural triggering
condition on file change) and concept-member→entity linkage (cf. **IDE-015**). This RFC
**names the boundary and defers the build** — the role grammar handles entity→entity;
non-entity targets are a separate, noted axis.

Per-edge disposition of the 5 `drift` rows (so "defer non-entity" doesn't hide the rest):

| drift row | intent | disposition |
|---|---|---|
| ×2 → memory entity | bears_on | **non-entity edge** (deferred — IMP-012/IDE-015) |
| ×1 → file path | bears_on | **non-entity edge** (deferred) |
| ×1 "carved out from" | decomposition | **D / `part_of`** (sibling spec) |
| ×1 feeds-into | sequence | **dep/seq layer** (`needs`/`after`/IMP-047), not the relation model |

Only the first 3 are non-entity; the latter 2 are entity→entity overload that B does not
resolve either — they route to D and the dep/seq layer respectively. `drift` is retired
once all four outlets exist; B alone does not empty it.

### Decomposition and altitude (Axis D — modelled here, built elsewhere)

Decomposition recurs at every altitude and is handled inconsistently today (`parent`
SPEC→SPEC only; nothing for PRD or work). It is **structural-first**, not reducible to
actionability:

- A single `part_of` primitive, **child→parent** (outbound-from-child, ADR-004-clean;
  inbound derived as `contains`/`parts`). The existing `parent` label (SPEC→SPEC)
  becomes a **specialization of `part_of`** — possibly carrying a `c4_level` facet — not
  a separate primitive; the migration folds it in.
- **`part_of` is kept strictly separate from reference-intent.** "Part of this IDE" is
  `references(scoped_from)→IDE`, **NOT** `part_of` — the work is *derived from part of*
  the idea, not structurally *a part of* it. Structural containment vs scoping-origin is
  the boundary; conflating them is the trap this split exists to prevent.
- `.doctrine/concept-map/001` already specifies the full lattice the relation graph
  lacks: a **product ladder** (Domain ⊃ Capability ⊃ Feature ⊃ User Story) and a
  **C4 ladder** (System Context ⊃ Container ⊃ Component ⊃ Code), declared *symmetric*.
  The `belongs to`/`decomposes` chains there are the **seed spec** for `part_of`.
- **Altitude is a target-side facet, not a role** (the design law): PRD carries
  `product_altitude`, SPEC carries `c4_level`. The work→artefact role stays
  altitude-agnostic; "implements a User Story vs contributes-to a Domain" reads off the
  target facet.
- The "is a" taxonomy (Feature *is a* PRD) is load-bearing but lives at the **type
  level, in the concept map** — exactly where a type ontology belongs. Hence
  `is_kind_of` is rejected at the **numbered-entity-instance** level (no crisp meaning
  distinct from `part_of`/`related`); `is_instance_of` is rejected (no class/template
  numbered kind exists); `composes_with` is rejected (collapses into
  `interactions`/`related`/`part_of`).

D is **carved to its own feature/spec**, not built in B's slice.

### Costs (eyes open)

1. Roles are a closed enum — each new intent is a code change (no worse than a new
   label today).
2. **Migration**: existing `specs`/`slices`/`related` edges need a role backfilled;
   `migrate` stamps a default (`implements`, or explicit `unspecified` to force triage).
   ~185 edges. **`unspecified` is migration-transient only — `role` is mandatory on every
   persisted `references` edge in steady state.** A durable `unspecified` would puncture
   the role-keyed target gate (it admits any target), so `validate` must flag it as
   unresolved, not accept it as a resting state.
3. **Storage/seam, not "one column".** The role threads the whole seam, today all
   label-keyed: `lookup(source,label)`, `RelationEdge`/`RelationRow { label, target }`,
   `validate_link`, plus the surfaces (`CatalogEdgeLabel`, `inspect`, `relation list`,
   web graph). "Adds one column to `RELATION_RULES`" is true of the *rule table* but
   understates the parser/storage/validator/CLI work — scope B accordingly.
4. **Open sub-decision**: role-derived inbound vs label-flat inbound (leaning
   role-derived). Mechanically this is not free: `inbound_name` is currently keyed by
   **label** and assumed identical across a label's rows; role-derived inbound requires
   `(label, role)` keying (one `references` label must render "implemented by" /
   "reviewed by" / "scoped from" / "bears on" / "related to"), and must coexist with the
   ADR-004 `superseded_by` reverse carve-out rather than duplicate it.

**Proposed direction** (for ratification — this RFC asserts no canon; adoption is via an
ADR/Revision + slices):

1. **Adopt the structure/intent split.** Introduce a closed **role** dimension on
   work→artefact relations, with target validation keyed on `(source, label, role)`.
   Collapse the work→canon family into `references` + `{implements, reviews,
   scoped_from, bears_on, related}`; keep `governed_by`, `part_of`, `supersedes`,
   `exclusive_with` (when demanded) as distinct labels.
   — *Shipped as `{implements, scoped_from, concerns}`: `concerns` absorbed
   `bears_on` + the lightweight `reviews` role; **`related` was kept as its own
   label, not collapsed**.*
2. **Adopt the *derivable, not relational* law** — coverage, temporal, and altitude are
   projections, not labels. `slices` stays one edge; planned/done reads off status.
   — *Shipped.*
3. **Keep graph-effect in the consumer**, not the vocabulary (consistent with IMP-047).
   — *Held (IMP-047 trinary actionability).*

**Settled** (CHR-024 design conversation, evidenced by P1):
- The label-vs-role principle and its boundary rulings — **with one reversal:
  `governed_by` kept (as proposed), but `related` was *not* collapsed** (the
  symmetry tension in the open list won; `related` stays a standalone label).
- Type safety is preserved under the collapse (role-indexed target gate). *Shipped.*
- The grammar is complete for the existing entity→entity population.

**Open / deferred** (named, not blocking):
- **Can work `implements` an ADR?** ADR is excluded from the `implements` target set for
  now — `governed_by` stays the clean ADR relation. But ADRs are sometimes authored
  eagerly and then *caught up* / enacted by a slice, which `governed_by` doesn't capture.
  The likelier resolution is that ADRs (like SPECs) **impose REQs**, and the REQ is the
  thing implemented — making `references(implements)→REQ` already the right edge and ADR
  never a direct target. Filed for later; not resolved here.
  **— Resolved 2026-06-29 (status quo): ADR is authority, not realisable canon; `members`
  is `{PRD,SPEC}`-only so the impose-REQs route does not exist; enactment is transitive
  (`implements`→SPEC `governed_by`→ADR). ADR stays `governed_by`-only. See Status.**
- **`requirements`-label fold** — the existing standalone `requirements` label (SL→REQ)
  collapses into `references(implements)→REQ`. Noted as explicit B scope, not implicit.
  **— Shipped (SL-149): `references(implements) → {SPEC,PRD,REQ}`.**
- Role-derived vs label-flat inbound (cost #4). **— Resolved: role-derived shipped**
  (`inbound_name` keyed `(label, role)`).
- The **non-entity-target edge** (memory/file/glob/vec) — IMP-012, IDE-015.
- **Altitude lattice + facets** — concept-map-001 is the seed; deferred to D.
- A **prose-hunt pass** for *absent* relations expressed as prose (governance-in-prose
  F-1, review-in-prose F-5, decomposition-in-prose F-7).
- **Relation *planes* — the `influences` family + `related`'s symmetry** (emergent,
  post-draft; partially reopens a settled ruling, so explicitly *not* locked). Two
  orthogonal axes surfaced after the snapshot: **directionality** (symmetric/double-ended
  vs one-sided) and **valence** (neutral vs agonistic−/assistive+). They imply two
  distinct *planes*:
  - `references`/`bears_on` sit on the **epistemic / design / governance** plane — how
    entities relate in the *knowledge* graph (this work implements that spec, this chore
    bears on that decision). Directional, neutral, role = intent-*verb*.
  - A candidate **`influences`** label sits on the **functional / technical** plane — how
    the *things themselves* affect each other. **Directional** ("A opposes — or assists —
    B without B influencing A"), and **valenced with no neutral center**: role carries
    *polarity*, `{opposes, conflicts, … enables, assists}`. Pure influence is never
    neutral — the neutral case is `related` or a `references` role, not influence. So
    `influences` does **not** subsume `bears_on` (different plane).
  - **`related` is symmetric + neutral** (double-ended, off the agonistic axis). This
    *tensions the settled `related → references(role=related)` collapse*: a symmetric
    relation as a role among directional `references` roles is the odd man out. **Open:**
    does `related` want its own symmetric-neutral label rather than a `references` role?
    Flagged, not relitigated here.
  - **`exclusive_with`** is symmetric + agonistic ("at most one of us") and therefore does
    **not** fit the directional `influences` family; its need is also uncertain. Deferred
    — modelled, not demanded by any current edge, and now also *unplaced* pending the
    plane/axis question.
- **Intra-record `shapes` — what does RECORD→RECORD mean?** The `shapes` label lets any
  knowledge record (ASM/DEC/QUE/CON) point at any other. Today that covers four seed
  records (CON-001 shapes QUE-001, ASM-001 shapes QUE-001, DEC-001 shapes QUE-001) —
  all semantically "this record *informs* or *constrains* the answer to that question."
  But `shapes` is the same label used for record→work (QUE-001 shapes SL-158) and
  record→gov (ASM-001 shapes RFC-008). Is the single `shapes` label correct for both
  record→work *gating* edges and record→record *epistemic* edges, or does the
  intra-record plane want a distinct label (or a role on `shapes`)? Tensions with the
  gating model (RFC-008): a record→record `shapes` edge must NOT gate (S4 — records are
  never workable, and dep-cycles are a hazard), so any blanket `shapes→gate` projection
  must exclude record→record. Open: does this call for a split, or is a record-target
  exclusion in the gating consumer sufficient? Filed here because it is a relation-model
  question, not an actionability question alone.
  **— Resolved by ADR-017 (accepted 2026-06-26, impl. SL-158): `shapes` is associative-only
  and feeds NO gate (CON-001: association ≠ gating); gating is `needs`-based on unsettled
  records. record→record `shapes` cannot gate, so no split/role/guard is needed. The premise
  ("any blanket `shapes→gate` projection") never materialised. See Status.**
