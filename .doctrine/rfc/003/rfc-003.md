## Provenance plane вЂ” open (folded 2026-06-29, IMP-207)

`drn doctor`'s lifecycle check surfaced 19 backlog items whose `slices` edges
(BACKLOGв†’SL = *addressed by*) actually encode the **reverse** causality: the item was
*created from* the slice (post-close finding, deferred scope, surfaced tech-debt). IMP-207
captures the fix. It is **`drift` redux** вЂ” a label abused because no structured outlet
exists (cf. В§"the boundary the role grammar cannot absorb"). Folded here as the
**provenance-plane exemplar**: a third concrete instance, after the `references` collapse
and the non-entity edge, of the closed-vocabulary gap this RFC exists to manage. (The
edge that opened this reconcile, IMP-095, is itself one of the 19.)

### The scattered origin axis

"Y originated from X" is already expressed by several bespoke labels at different
coordinates, none aware of the others:

| edge | source в†’ target | reciprocal | meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `spawns` | RECORD в†’ backlog | `spawned_by` | a knowledge record spawned this work item (SPEC-019 D6) |
| `references(scoped_from)` | SL в†’ backlog | `scoped into` | this slice was scoped from that item |
| `spawned_from` *(IMP-207, proposed)* | BACKLOG в†’ SL | *(tbd)* | this item was born from that slice |
| `drift` "carved out from" | BACKLOG в†’ free-text | вЂ” | decomposition origin (RFC В§drift disposition, Г—1) |

These are one **plane** вЂ” provenance/origin вЂ” surfacing as four point-labels. This RFC
named two relation planes (epistemic vs functional `influences`); provenance is a **third**
it did not name.

### The deciding question

Per В§"the deciding principle вЂ” label vs role": is `spawned_from` a **new label**, a **role**
on a generalized origin label, or evidence the model wants a first-class **provenance
plane** (a label + closed role/source-set grammar, the way `references` unified the
workв†’canon axis)?

- **(a) Point label.** Mint `spawned_from` (BACKLOGв†’SL) as-is. Cheapest; repeats the
  per-intent-a-label accretion flagged as cost #1. The plane stays implicit, and the next
  origin coordinate (SPECв†’backlog, ADRв†’backlog) mints another.
- **(b) Generalize `spawns`.** Widen `spawns`' source-set beyond RECORD so any origin
  (slice, spec) spawns a work item; inbound stays `spawned_by`. **Blocked by
  directionality** вЂ” `spawns` authors *outbound at the origin* (ADR-004), but a slice
  origin is usually **closed**, so the edge would require editing a terminal artefact.
  IMP-207 chose BACKLOGв†’SL precisely so the *live* end authors. A generalized `spawns` and
  a `spawned_from` are therefore **not** the same edge reversed.
- **(c) Provenance plane.** A single origin label refined by role + a source/target
  grammar вЂ” the `references` pattern applied to provenance. Most coherent; most work; needs
  the directionality rule (author at the mutable end) as a first-class property no existing
  axis has.

### Constraints the decision must honour

- **Outbound-only (ADR-004) + author-at-the-mutable-end.** The second is *not* a modelled
  property today. Provenance is the first axis where the natural author is dictated by
  lifecycle state, not by a fixed source kind вЂ” possibly the genuinely new thing the model
  must absorb.
- **Graph-effect (Layer 1).** `addressed_by`-terminal в†’ should-be-terminal;
  `spawned_from`-terminal в†’ expected. The `doctor` projection must read provenance edges as
  origin, not implementation вЂ” the warning's correctness depends on it.
- **Retcon is independent.** The 19-edge relabel (IMP-207 step 2) proceeds regardless of
  (a)/(b)/(c); only the *label it relabels to* waits on this decision.

## Context

Doctrine's entity-relationship model grew incrementally вЂ” SL-046 (reader), SL-048
(writer + tier-1 migration), SL-095 (governance supersession), SL-136 (tags), and
ad-hoc extensions вЂ” governed by ADR-004 (outbound-only) and ADR-010 (tier partition +
contract unification), with SPEC-018 capturing the cross-corpus contract. It has never
been reviewed **holistically**. CHR-024 drives that review; this RFC is its
deliberation deliverable.

The live vocabulary is **20 outbound labels** in one const table
(`RELATION_RULES`, `src/relation.rs`), each declaring `(sources, label) в†’ target В·
tier В· link-policy В· inbound_name`. The model lavished structure on **lineage**
(`descends_from`/`parent`/`members`/`interactions`, four tier-2 labels) and on
**knowledge records** (`shapes`/`spawns`, reaching 16 kinds), while the busiest
real-world traffic вЂ” **work в†’ artefact** вЂ” got exactly one overloaded label per
direction (`specs`, `slices`). That asymmetry is the problem.

### The four axes (CHR-024 findings, triaged)

The seven preliminary findings (F-1..F-7) plus the friction backlog items collapse to
four axes at different altitudes:

| axis | problem | disposition |
|---|---|---|
| **A** backlog second-class | `governed_by`/`related`/`reviews` exclude BACKLOG sources (F-1/F-2/F-4/F-5) | **easy win вЂ” slice now**, source-set extension; need not wait on this RFC |
| **B** overloaded edge intent | `specs`/`slices`/`related` conflate distinct intents (F-3, IMP-149) | **this RFC's purpose** |
| **C** coverage / completion | no partial-satisfaction, premature-close risk (F-6, IMP-141) | **not a vocabulary problem** в†’ `validate`/`/close`/SPEC-002 |
| **D** decomposition / hierarchy | no slice-split, no epic, no altitude lattice (F-7) | **carve out as a sibling feature/spec**; its modelling informs B |

### The question

> Should doctrine separate **durable structural relation shape** from **contextual
> role-intent** вЂ” keeping a small primitive vocabulary refined by a closed role
> grammar вЂ” while leaving actionability, priority, coverage, and closure to their
> owning consumers?

## Discussion

### Empirical grounding (P1 classification)

Before theorising, the B-relevant edge population was classified exhaustively from
`doctrine relation list` (authoritative, all `resolved`). **Snapshot вЂ” P1 census, 2026-06-23:**
939 edges total; B-relevant = `specs` (60) + `related` (48) + `drift` (5) + `slices`
(77, temporal); classified population = **113** (`specs`+`related`+`drift`).

> **Snapshot caveat.** The corpus grows; later censuses drift. As of a re-run while
> reviewing this RFC the live counts were ~951 total, `related` **60** (the classified
> figure above was 48), so the `specs`+`related`+`drift` population is ~125, not 113.
> The ~12 newer `related` edges are **unclassified** вЂ” the completeness verdict below is
> "role-complete **as of the snapshot**," not a standing guarantee. Re-classify before
> treating these counts as live proof.

Classified against a candidate role grammar `{implements, reviews, scoped_from,
bears_on, related}`:

- **The grammar is complete for entityв†’entity edges** *as classified at the snapshot*.
  It covers **100%** of the 113 `specs`/`related`/`drift` edges then resolved. No edge
  demands a role outside the set. (Caveat: 3 of
  the 5 `drift` edges target memory/file вЂ” the role is assignable but the *target* is
  not expressible until the non-entity edge ships; see the boundary section. "Complete"
  here means role-complete, not target-complete.)
- **The `reviews` *role* has zero instances in the reference family** вЂ” and must not be
  confused with the live first-class **`reviews` label** (`RV в†’ AnyNumbered`,
  `Tier::Typed`, `TypedVerbOnly`, ~145 edges in `RELATION_RULES`), which is the
  structured RV review-subject edge and is **NOT** collapsed into `references`. The
  reference-family `reviews` role is empty precisely *because* reviewing is already a
  first-class typed edge carried by RV records; the role is reserved for a *non-RV*
  reviewer outlet (BACKLOG/SL в†’ target, Axis A) and is otherwise redundant with the RV
  typed edge. Absent-edge, not refuted.
- **The current labels mismap intent at scale** вЂ” the grammar *corrects*, not merely
  renames:
  - `specs`: `SLв†’` = implements (~44, fine); **`IMPв†’`/`RSKв†’` mean bears_on/scoped_from**
    (12 mismapped вЂ” a risk cannot *implement* a spec; an improvement's spawned slice
    implements, the improvement itself only bears on).
  - `related`: the peer reading is the **minority** вЂ” `RFCв†’bag` = bears_on (~26),
    `SLв†’backlog` = **scoped_from** (~13), `GOVв†’GOV` = pure related (4), `SLв†’SL` =
    peer/seq (4).
  - `drift` (5): a free-text escape hatch holding bears_on (Г—3), decomposition (Г—1,
    "carved out from" = F-7 in the wild), and sequence (Г—1).
- **`exclusive_with`: zero instances** вЂ” genuinely speculative.
- **`slices`: 100% BACKLOGв†’SL, temporal** вЂ” see the design law below.

~25 of ~113 edges assert an intent their label gets wrong. This is not cosmetic.

### Layer 1 вЂ” structure is not graph-effect

Whether an edge *gates work*, *evicts an alternative*, or *scores consequence* is a
**consumer decision**, not a property of the relation. `A part_of B` is structurally
true regardless of whether the priority engine cares. So:

- **Relation contract** (`RELATION_RULES`): structural truth вЂ” sources, target, tier,
  role grammar. Durable.
- **Consumer policy** (priority overlay / `/close` / `validate`): graph-effect, gating,
  eviction, closure. A *projection* over selected edges.

A label's gating behaviour is **declared in the consumer**, not the vocabulary вЂ”
IMP-047 (trinary actionability) is the principled version of that assignment (today a
new label silently gets `Reject,Unbounded` until the overlay assigns policy).
Actionability (`needs`/`after`/IMP-047 `gates`) belongs to the dep/seq layer, not the
semantic relation model.

### Layer 2 вЂ” the design law: *derivable, not relational*

> **Do not encode in the relation what is derivable from entity state or facets.** The
> edge carries durable structural intent; status, coverage, and altitude *project* over
> it.

This law surfaced three times вЂ” three "apparent missing labels" that are really
projections:

- **Coverage (C)** вЂ” not a label в†’ `validate` / `/close` / SPEC-002. Corollary:
  `partially_addresses` is a **C-predicate, explicitly kept OUT of the role grammar** вЂ”
  partial satisfaction is a coverage projection, not a structural intent.
- **Temporal (`slices` planned-vs-done, IMP-149)** вЂ” not a label в†’ derive from the
  target slice's **lifecycle status**. Minting `planned_by`/`completed_by` is a category
  error: it forces *rewriting the edge as status moves*. The edge is stable; the status
  moves. This dissolves the structural half of IMP-149 at **zero** vocabulary cost.
  *Caveat:* "open = planned, closed = done" is only sound if the lifecycleв†’reading map is
  normative вЂ” closure-without-delivery states (cancelled / superseded / abandoned) must
  map to their own reading, not silently to "done." The projection is deferred to
  lifecycle semantics; the claim is "no new label," not "the mapping is trivial."
- **Altitude (SLв†’PRD vs SLв†’SPEC)** вЂ” not a role в†’ derive from the target's altitude
  **facet** (see Decomposition below).

### The deciding principle вЂ” label vs role

> **Separate LABEL** when the distinction changes the edge's **structure, graph-effect,
> or inbound semantics.**
> **ROLE facet** when it only refines the **intent** of an otherwise
> structurally-identical edge.

Applied:

| candidate | differs inвЂ¦ | verdict |
|---|---|---|
| implements / reviews / scoped_from / bears_on | intent only (same structure: workв†’canon, consequence-scored, no gate) | **roles** on `references` |
| `part_of` | structure: hierarchy, tree render, close-gate | **label** |
| `supersedes` | lifecycle + reverse carve-out | **label** |
| `exclusive_with` | symmetric + (future) evict | **label** |
| `governed_by` | authority semantics + distinct "governs" inbound | **label** (stays) |
| `related` | weakest peer вЂ” the degenerate no-intent reference | **collapses** в†’ `references(role=related)` |

### Type safety survives the collapse

The objection to a generic `references` label is that it sacrifices the target-kind
gate (`specs в†’ {PRD,SPEC}`) that the validator enforces today. It does not вЂ” the gate
**relocates from the label to the role**:

- Move target validation from `(source, label) в†’ TargetSpec` to
  `(source, label, role) в†’ TargetSpec`.
- **Two-level closed grammar:**
  - `(source_kind, label) в†’ legal roles` вЂ” e.g. `references` from SL admits
    `{implements, reviews, scoped_from, bears_on}`; from RV admits `{reviews}` only.
  - `(source_kind, label, role) в†’ TargetSpec` вЂ” e.g. `references(implements) в†’
    {SPEC,PRD,REQ}`; `references(reviews) в†’ AnyNumbered`. (Target sets are
    corpus-proven: `specsв†’{PRD,SPEC}` and the separate `requirementsв†’{REQ}` label fold
    in here. **ADR is deliberately excluded** вЂ” see the open question below; ADR
    authority stays on `governed_by`.)
- **Roles are a closed enum**, not free text.

The *rule table* is not new machinery: `RELATION_RULES` is *already* multi-row per label
(`related` Г—2, `supersedes` Г—3); it gains one column, and the lockstep enum-order test
and overlay allocation survive unchanged. (The *seam* beyond the table вЂ” storage row,
`lookup`, `validate_link`, surfaces вЂ” is real work; see Costs #3.)

`specs`'s real defect was a **noun verbing** вЂ” the missing verb *is* the role. Once the
role supplies the verb, the target set widens to actual canon and becomes
role-determined. "These 3 REQs" = three `references(implements)в†’REQ`; "this entire PRD"
= `references(implements)в†’PRD`.

A property falls out for free: because the role names the verb, the **inbound reciprocal
is role-derived** вЂ” `inspect SPEC-018` reads "implemented by SL-x В· reviewed by CHR-024
В· scoped SL-y" instead of a flat "specs Г—3". (Whether to take this vs label-flat inbound
is the one sub-decision left open.)

### The boundary the role grammar cannot absorb

P1 found `drift` pointing at **memory entities (Г—2) and a file path (Г—1)** вЂ” targets
*outside the numbered-entity space*. The `references`+role grammar is **entityв†’entity**;
it does not cover backlogв†’memory or backlogв†’file. `drift` is abused precisely because no
structured outlet exists. A future edge shape is needed:

```
(label + role)[src: entity] в†’ [target: non-entity | file | glob | vec-of-files]
```

This is the home for **IMP-012** (architectural triggers вЂ” structural triggering
condition on file change) and concept-memberв†’entity linkage (cf. **IDE-015**). This RFC
**names the boundary and defers the build** вЂ” the role grammar handles entityв†’entity;
non-entity targets are a separate, noted axis.

Per-edge disposition of the 5 `drift` rows (so "defer non-entity" doesn't hide the rest):

| drift row | intent | disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Г—2 в†’ memory entity | bears_on | **non-entity edge** (deferred вЂ” IMP-012/IDE-015) |
| Г—1 в†’ file path | bears_on | **non-entity edge** (deferred) |
| Г—1 "carved out from" | decomposition | **D / `part_of`** (sibling spec) |
| Г—1 feeds-into | sequence | **dep/seq layer** (`needs`/`after`/IMP-047), not the relation model |

Only the first 3 are non-entity; the latter 2 are entityв†’entity overload that B does not
resolve either вЂ” they route to D and the dep/seq layer respectively. `drift` is retired
once all four outlets exist; B alone does not empty it.

### Decomposition and altitude (Axis D вЂ” modelled here, built elsewhere)

Decomposition recurs at every altitude and is handled inconsistently today (`parent`
SPECв†’SPEC only; nothing for PRD or work). It is **structural-first**, not reducible to
actionability:

- A single `part_of` primitive, **childв†’parent** (outbound-from-child, ADR-004-clean;
  inbound derived as `contains`/`parts`). The existing `parent` label (SPECв†’SPEC)
  becomes a **specialization of `part_of`** вЂ” possibly carrying a `c4_level` facet вЂ” not
  a separate primitive; the migration folds it in.
- **`part_of` is kept strictly separate from reference-intent.** "Part of this IDE" is
  `references(scoped_from)в†’IDE`, **NOT** `part_of` вЂ” the work is *derived from part of*
  the idea, not structurally *a part of* it. Structural containment vs scoping-origin is
  the boundary; conflating them is the trap this split exists to prevent.
- `.doctrine/concept-map/001` already specifies the full lattice the relation graph
  lacks: a **product ladder** (Domain вЉѓ Capability вЉѓ Feature вЉѓ User Story) and a
  **C4 ladder** (System Context вЉѓ Container вЉѓ Component вЉѓ Code), declared *symmetric*.
  The `belongs to`/`decomposes` chains there are the **seed spec** for `part_of`.
- **Altitude is a target-side facet, not a role** (the design law): PRD carries
  `product_altitude`, SPEC carries `c4_level`. The workв†’artefact role stays
  altitude-agnostic; "implements a User Story vs contributes-to a Domain" reads off the
  target facet.
- The "is a" taxonomy (Feature *is a* PRD) is load-bearing but lives at the **type
  level, in the concept map** вЂ” exactly where a type ontology belongs. Hence
  `is_kind_of` is rejected at the **numbered-entity-instance** level (no crisp meaning
  distinct from `part_of`/`related`); `is_instance_of` is rejected (no class/template
  numbered kind exists); `composes_with` is rejected (collapses into
  `interactions`/`related`/`part_of`).

D is **carved to its own feature/spec**, not built in B's slice.

### Costs (eyes open)

1. Roles are a closed enum вЂ” each new intent is a code change (no worse than a new
   label today).
2. **Migration**: existing `specs`/`slices`/`related` edges need a role backfilled;
   `migrate` stamps a default (`implements`, or explicit `unspecified` to force triage).
   ~185 edges. **`unspecified` is migration-transient only вЂ” `role` is mandatory on every
   persisted `references` edge in steady state.** A durable `unspecified` would puncture
   the role-keyed target gate (it admits any target), so `validate` must flag it as
   unresolved, not accept it as a resting state.
3. **Storage/seam, not "one column".** The role threads the whole seam, today all
   label-keyed: `lookup(source,label)`, `RelationEdge`/`RelationRow { label, target }`,
   `validate_link`, plus the surfaces (`CatalogEdgeLabel`, `inspect`, `relation list`,
   web graph). "Adds one column to `RELATION_RULES`" is true of the *rule table* but
   understates the parser/storage/validator/CLI work вЂ” scope B accordingly.
4. **Open sub-decision**: role-derived inbound vs label-flat inbound (leaning
   role-derived). Mechanically this is not free: `inbound_name` is currently keyed by
   **label** and assumed identical across a label's rows; role-derived inbound requires
   `(label, role)` keying (one `references` label must render "implemented by" /
   "reviewed by" / "scoped from" / "bears on" / "related to"), and must coexist with the
   ADR-004 `superseded_by` reverse carve-out rather than duplicate it.

**Proposed direction** (for ratification вЂ” this RFC asserts no canon; adoption is via an
ADR/Revision + slices):

1. **Adopt the structure/intent split.** Introduce a closed **role** dimension on
   workв†’artefact relations, with target validation keyed on `(source, label, role)`.
   Collapse the workв†’canon family into `references` + `{implements, reviews,
   scoped_from, bears_on, related}`; keep `governed_by`, `part_of`, `supersedes`,
   `exclusive_with` (when demanded) as distinct labels.
   вЂ” *Shipped as `{implements, scoped_from, concerns}`: `concerns` absorbed
   `bears_on` + the lightweight `reviews` role; **`related` was kept as its own
   label, not collapsed**.*
2. **Adopt the *derivable, not relational* law** вЂ” coverage, temporal, and altitude are
   projections, not labels. `slices` stays one edge; planned/done reads off status.
   вЂ” *Shipped.*
3. **Keep graph-effect in the consumer**, not the vocabulary (consistent with IMP-047).
   вЂ” *Held (IMP-047 trinary actionability).*

**Settled** (CHR-024 design conversation, evidenced by P1):
- The label-vs-role principle and its boundary rulings вЂ” **with one reversal:
  `governed_by` kept (as proposed), but `related` was *not* collapsed** (the
  symmetry tension in the open list won; `related` stays a standalone label).
- Type safety is preserved under the collapse (role-indexed target gate). *Shipped.*
- The grammar is complete for the existing entityв†’entity population.

**Open / deferred** (named, not blocking):
- **Can work `implements` an ADR?** ADR is excluded from the `implements` target set for
  now вЂ” `governed_by` stays the clean ADR relation. But ADRs are sometimes authored
  eagerly and then *caught up* / enacted by a slice, which `governed_by` doesn't capture.
  The likelier resolution is that ADRs (like SPECs) **impose REQs**, and the REQ is the
  thing implemented вЂ” making `references(implements)в†’REQ` already the right edge and ADR
  never a direct target. Filed for later; not resolved here.
- **`requirements`-label fold** вЂ” the existing standalone `requirements` label (SLв†’REQ)
  collapses into `references(implements)в†’REQ`. Noted as explicit B scope, not implicit.
  **вЂ” Shipped (SL-149): `references(implements) в†’ {SPEC,PRD,REQ}`.**
- Role-derived vs label-flat inbound (cost #4). **вЂ” Resolved: role-derived shipped**
  (`inbound_name` keyed `(label, role)`).
- The **non-entity-target edge** (memory/file/glob/vec) вЂ” IMP-012, IDE-015.
- **Altitude lattice + facets** вЂ” concept-map-001 is the seed; deferred to D.
- A **prose-hunt pass** for *absent* relations expressed as prose (governance-in-prose
  F-1, review-in-prose F-5, decomposition-in-prose F-7).
- **Relation *planes* вЂ” the `influences` family + `related`'s symmetry** (emergent,
  post-draft; partially reopens a settled ruling, so explicitly *not* locked). Two
  orthogonal axes surfaced after the snapshot: **directionality** (symmetric/double-ended
  vs one-sided) and **valence** (neutral vs agonisticв€’/assistive+). They imply two
  distinct *planes*:
  - `references`/`bears_on` sit on the **epistemic / design / governance** plane вЂ” how
    entities relate in the *knowledge* graph (this work implements that spec, this chore
    bears on that decision). Directional, neutral, role = intent-*verb*.
  - A candidate **`influences`** label sits on the **functional / technical** plane вЂ” how
    the *things themselves* affect each other. **Directional** ("A opposes вЂ” or assists вЂ”
    B without B influencing A"), and **valenced with no neutral center**: role carries
    *polarity*, `{opposes, conflicts, вЂ¦ enables, assists}`. Pure influence is never
    neutral вЂ” the neutral case is `related` or a `references` role, not influence. So
    `influences` does **not** subsume `bears_on` (different plane).
  - **`related` is symmetric + neutral** (double-ended, off the agonistic axis). This
    *tensions the settled `related в†’ references(role=related)` collapse*: a symmetric
    relation as a role among directional `references` roles is the odd man out. **Open:**
    does `related` want its own symmetric-neutral label rather than a `references` role?
    Flagged, not relitigated here.
  - **`exclusive_with`** is symmetric + agonistic ("at most one of us") and therefore does
    **not** fit the directional `influences` family; its need is also uncertain. Deferred
    вЂ” modelled, not demanded by any current edge, and now also *unplaced* pending the
    plane/axis question.
- **Intra-record `shapes` вЂ” what does RECORDв†’RECORD mean?** The `shapes` label lets any
  knowledge record (ASM/DEC/QUE/CON) point at any other. Today that covers four seed
  records (CON-001 shapes QUE-001, ASM-001 shapes QUE-001, DEC-001 shapes QUE-001) вЂ”
  all semantically "this record *informs* or *constrains* the answer to that question."
  But `shapes` is the same label used for recordв†’work (QUE-001 shapes SL-158) and
  recordв†’gov (ASM-001 shapes RFC-008). Is the single `shapes` label correct for both
  recordв†’work *gating* edges and recordв†’record *epistemic* edges, or does the
  intra-record plane want a distinct label (or a role on `shapes`)? Tensions with the
  gating model (RFC-008): a recordв†’record `shapes` edge must NOT gate (S4 вЂ” records are
  never workable, and dep-cycles are a hazard), so any blanket `shapesв†’gate` projection
  must exclude recordв†’record. Open: does this call for a split, or is a record-target
  exclusion in the gating consumer sufficient? Filed here because it is a relation-model
  question, not an actionability question alone.
