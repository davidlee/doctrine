# RFC-001: Thesis: graph value is gated on consumption surfaces

## Context

Doctrine's graph topology is rich on the inside and thin on the outside. The internal
model is mature and well-governed — corpus FK/orphan clean (`spec validate`: "corpus
clean"), all 47 spec lineage edges resolve, ADR-001 layering enforced by a
whole-crate fitness test, the relation contract (SPEC-018) precise, and the client
is a thin renderer (no server-policy duplication). The *capture* and *storage* of
topology is essentially done.

But every recurring gap identified across a session's analysis — proposals 0003,
0006, 0007, 0009, 0011 — shares a common shape: the gap is on the **consumption /
outward surface**, not on the internal model. What can a human, team, or external
tool *do* with the graph?

This thesis names the pattern: the graph becomes indispensable to teams at the
moment it (a) answers "what does my change touch?" (transitive impact query),
(b) keeps the human's diagram honest (concept-map bridge), (c) leaves the tool
into Gephi/Neo4j/dashboards (graph interchange), (d) lets a human assert priority
over the derived order (authored priority slot), and (e) gives one trustworthy
"is the graph sound?" gate for CI (unified doctor). None of these need new
*modelling* — they need new *surfaces* on the model that exists.

## Discussion

### The surface evidence

Five proposals trace the same fault line:

- **0003 (transitive impact query):** `inspect` is 1-hop only, yet `blockers
  --transitive` and `retrieve --expand N` prove the cordage walk exists. The walk
  is there; the end-user verb is not.
- **0006 (concept-map bridge):** concept maps (the human narrative view) are
  decoupled from the real relation graph — no validation, no drift detection.
- **0007 (graph interchange export):** `export` ships only the agent-facing
  lazyspec; no DOT/GraphML/Cypher for the tools teams already run.
- **0009 (authored priority slot):** SPEC-001 D10 is designed and REQ-054
  requirement-backed but unbuilt and untracked — the human's "pin this" lever
  over the derived worklist.
- **0011 (unified doctor):** no single `doctrine doctor` health gate; integrity
  checks exist across four commands a team must know to assemble.

By contrast, most open *backlog* improvements push the other way — deeper internal
modelling (IMP-053 record↔record, IMP-069/070/097 altitude validation, IMP-095/063
supersession vocab, IMP-105 lazyspec projection coverage). Valuable, but they
refine a model that already works; they don't move the needle on "indispensable
to product teams." The marginal return on more internal correctness appears lower
than the marginal return on *exposing what's already correct*.

### Options

1. **"Consumption surfaces first" theme** — prioritise 0003/0006/0007/0009/0011
   ahead of internal-modelling backlog. Maximises movement on the
   team-indispensable axis; defers internal refinements that may be prerequisites
   for some surfaces (e.g. IMP-020 cordage-walk unification gates 0003).

2. **Sequence by dependency** — let prerequisites pull order (IMP-020 → 0003;
   SPEC-018 spine/0005 → richer export). Cleaner build order, but risks staying
   in internal-modelling indefinitely because there's always one more prerequisite.

3. **No change** — treat each proposal independently on its own merits. Loses the
   strategic lens; the consumption-surface pattern is the actual product signal
   and dropping it means re-deriving it per-item.

### Recommended path

Option 1 with a dependency caveat: make "consumption surfaces" the explicit next
theme, and within it respect the few real prerequisites (IMP-020 before 0003; the
`/api/graph` serialization is already the reuse seam for 0007, so it has none).
The internal model is the asset; its ROI is currently trapped because little can
be *done* with it from outside. The cheapest, highest-leverage moves are all
surfaces, and several reuse existing seams (cordage `reachable`, `/api/graph`,
the D10 comparator) rather than new machinery.

## Outcome

Open — discussion record for deliberation on the prioritisation thesis. The
recommendation is to adopt Option 1 with the dependency caveat; decision deferred.
