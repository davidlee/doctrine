# ADR-013: Revision as a first-class change-axis kind; governance dependency routes through a Revision

## Context

Two backlog items name the same gap from opposite ends. **IDE-003** wants a
vehicle to *stage and approve* requirement/spec-prose deltas before they land.
**IDE-010** wants a *work-lifecycle* entity that captures pending intent to revise
governance, so a slice can `needs` it instead of `needs`-ing an evergreen doc.

Doctrine has no home for either. `REV-` exists only as a **deferred spec subtype**
(`doc/spec-entity-spec.md` § Out of scope), and `doc/entity-model.md` § Adjudication
left its home **open** — "it may resolve to a spec facet or a slice outcome" —
while nudging it change-side. The open question blocks the unification: until
`REV-`'s home is decided, neither IDE-003 nor IDE-010 can be built without
prejudicing the other.

Two forces decide it:

- **The SL-060 gradient-inversion invariant.** Evergreen governance/spec docs are
  *not* work; admitting them as `needs`/`after` dep-seq targets inverts the
  gradient (durable canon made to depend on transient work). Work that must wait on
  a governance change therefore needs a **work-lifecycle anchor** to depend on — an
  entity that is born, worked, and settled — not the doc itself.
- **ADR-009 "approval is not lifecycle."** A vehicle that stages truth-writes and
  gates them on approval must keep approval as a field orthogonal to status, not as
  a lifecycle state. A spec *subtype* (identity-side, status-less by the spec model)
  cannot carry a work lifecycle; a *slice facet* would fuse the revise-intent into a
  single slice's FSM and lose the standalone anchor IDE-010 needs.

A spec subtype is identity-side and the wrong axis; a slice facet is non-standalone.
Both fail the two forces. The home that satisfies both is the **change axis**,
alongside slice and REC.

## Decision

**Revision (`REV-NNN`) is a first-class, standalone change-axis kind with a work
lifecycle** — sibling to slice and REC on the change axis, but lifecycle-shaped
like work rather than immutable like REC. **Not** a spec subtype and **not** a
slice facet. It is one entity unifying IDE-003 and IDE-010, seen at two lifecycle lenses:
born as content-light pending intent (dependents anchor on it from birth), it
accumulates staged deltas as it is worked, is approved on the ADR-009 conduct axis,
then **applied** (deltas land) and settled.

**Governance→work dependency routes through a Revision.** Work that must wait on a
governance or spec change declares `needs REV-NNN` — never `needs` the evergreen doc
itself. Evergreen governance/spec docs remain excluded as dep-seq targets (the
SL-060 invariant holds); the Revision is the work-lifecycle anchor that stands in
for the pending change. This is the IDE-010 payoff: a crisp single anchor a slice
can depend on, born before any delta is staged.

**Approval is a separate field, enforced at automated apply and expected before
manual landing.** Per ADR-009, approval is orthogonal to status (lifecycle
transitions are approval-blind and advisory). In v1, `revision apply` refuses
unless `approval=approved` for the **auto-applied subset** (the `status` rows — the
one part with a genuine engine seam); `introduce`/`create`/`modify`/`move`/prose
rows are **surfaced-for-manual** and land under the authored-truth honour model
named in the SL-066 design. The apply checkpoint is the **forcing-function** that
requires recorded approval before the automated truth-write. Consistent with
ADR-009's invoker-blind posture, it is not actor-attributed authz (a solo dev
self-approves); identity-gated enforcement remains the ADR-009 OQ-1 follow-up.

This ADR records the **decision and its home** only. The entity's mechanism — the
lifecycle vocabulary, the typed `[[change]]` payload, the relation contract, the
dep/seq wiring, and the apply path — is the SL-066 design (`design.md`), not
restated here.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The open `REV-` home question (`entity-model.md` § Adjudication) closes: a
  standalone change-axis kind, not a spec subtype, not a slice facet.
- IDE-003 and IDE-010 unify behind one entity at each's strength — the crisp
  dependency anchor and the staged-delta payload — instead of two parallel features.
- The SL-060 invariant is preserved *and* made useful: governance dependency has a
  sanctioned route (through the Revision) rather than an excluded dead end.
- Approval stays on the ADR-009 conduct axis (a field, not a state); the apply
  checkpoint expresses "approval is not lifecycle" as a single deliberate gate.

### Negative

- A new kind adds corpus-walk surface: every `inspect` now also walks `revision/`
  (additive cost, RSK-006-class), and the `KINDS` row must be consumed by all of its
  walk tables or the wiring half-works (a SL-066 execution constraint, design §4.7).
- The change axis now carries three kinds (slice, REC, Revision); a reader must hold
  the distinction (slice = work; REC = immutable reconcile record; Revision = staged
  pending change).
- Manual-row Revisions can unblock dependents on operator assertion, not machine
  verification: `done` requires every row landed, but nothing checks the
  surfaced-for-manual subset before `done` is set, and `needs REV-NNN` unblocks on
  it. Acceptable for v1, but must stay visible in `done` semantics and verification
  copy (SL-066 design § Non-Goals).

### Neutral

- Net-new ADR — supersedes no prior ADR (the accepted set is unchanged in force),
  but closes the local `REV-` home question in `entity-model.md` and relocates the
  deferred spec-subtype note in `spec-entity-spec.md`.
- Does not alter REC, the spec model, or the slice FSM; the Revision orchestrates
  existing write seams at apply (no parallel implementation — ADR-010 grain).
- v1 enforcement is deliberately partial (approval checkpoint is invoker-blind;
  surfaced-for-manual rows land on the operator's word) — the same authored-truth
  trust model as ADR-009 and SL-044, named in the SL-066 design § Non-Goals.

## Verification

- `doc/entity-model.md` § Adjudication no longer leaves `REV-`'s home open — it
  resolves to a standalone change-axis kind and cites this ADR.
- `doc/spec-entity-spec.md` no longer frames `REV-` as a deferred spec subtype — it
  relocates `REV-` off the spec family and cites this ADR.
- Dep/seq verification (executed in the SL-066 code phases) proves the core
  behavioural rule: `needs REV-NNN` is accepted, a terminal REV unblocks its
  dependents, and `needs ADR/SPEC/POL/STD` remains refused (the SL-060 invariant).
- The SL-066 design and plan descend from this decision; the kind, payload, dep/seq
  wiring, and apply path are verified there (the code phases), not in this ADR.
