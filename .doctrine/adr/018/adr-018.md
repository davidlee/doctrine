# ADR-018: Finish Axis B: neutral originates_from provenance, fulfils label with completion degree, value-burndown

## Context

RFC-003's Axis B ("overloaded edge intent") had two halves. **ADR-016 + SL-149 finished
the work‚Üícanon half** ‚Äî the `specs`/`requirements` noun-labels collapsed onto one
`references` label refined by a closed `Role`. They left the work‚Üí**backlog** half
standing: **`slices`** (BACKLOG‚ÜíSL, "addressed by") and **`drift`** (BACKLOG‚Üífree-text).
`slices` conflates three dimensions in one binary edge ‚Äî **provenance** (who was born from
whom), **fulfilment** (this slice does the item's work), and **completion** (how much).

ADR-016 deliberately deferred this half. Its Decision ¬ß2 (the "derivable-not-relational
law") ruled completion a *projection*, not a relation ‚Äî "`slices` stays one edge;
planned/done reads off status" ‚Äî and named coverage, temporal planned-vs-done, and
altitude as projections too. That ruling was correct for what it surveyed; finishing
Axis B surfaced a forcing fact it did not have.

**The forcing fact (SL-176 design, R10).** Flipping `slices` (item‚ÜíSL) to a slice-authored
`fulfils` (SL‚Üíitem) is *not* behaviour-preserving for priority: the consequence pass credits
the edge **target ‚àù the source's base** (`graph.rs:595-628`), so reversing the edge flips
the credited node. And completion is genuinely **relational** ‚Äî a `done` slice may only
*partially* satisfy an item; that fact lives on the (slice, item) pair, recoverable from
neither endpoint's lifecycle. RFC-003's Axis C completion hole, which ADR-016 closed by
projection, re-opens here. RFC-003 asserts no canon; this ADR is its ratification for the
finish-Axis-B half (the work‚Üícanon half stays ratified by ADR-016).

## Decision

We finish Axis B by retiring `slices` into the universal grammar, splitting the three
conflated dimensions across one neutral role, one new label, and one facet.

**1. Provenance ‚Üí one neutral `references(originates_from)` role.** Rename
`Role::ScopedFrom` ‚Üí `Role::OriginatesFrom` in place (wire `scoped_from` ‚Üí
`originates_from`; inbound "scoped into" ‚Üí "originated from"). Widen its sources `{SL}` ‚Üí
`{SL + backlog kinds}` and targets `Kinds(BACKLOG)` ‚Üí `Kinds(BACKLOG + SL)`. One neutral
"born from" role subsumes the old `scoped_from` *and* IMP-207's proposed `spawned_from`:
a backlog item authors "born from SL", a slice authors "born from idea / sibling slice".
The parallel with the existing REV‚ÜíRFC `originates_from` **label** (SL-122) is accepted ‚Äî
different namespaces (`label=` vs `role=`), same English meaning, distinct tiers.

**2. Fulfilment ‚Üí a new `fulfils` label** (SL ‚Üí backlog), retiring `slices`. The old
`slices` "addressed by" reading becomes `fulfils`' **derived inbound** ("fulfilled by") ‚Äî
`backlog show` renders it through the same inbound machinery `inspect` uses, becoming
corpus-aware (ADR-004-consistent: inbound is always derived). Author-at-mutable-end is held
by convention plus the SL-only source-set, which structurally fences authorship to the
slice end.

**3. Completion ‚Üí a `{full, partial}` degree facet** ‚Äî a non-keyed `Option<Degree>` column
on the `fulfils` edge, `None ‚â° Full`. **This partially reverses ADR-016 ¬ß2's
derivable-not-relational law, for completion only.** Completion is per-(slice, item) data
that no endpoint's status carries; it is relational, not a projection. The reversal is
deliberately narrow: degree **keys no gate** (unlike `Role`, it never enters the target
lookup) and **does not aggregate** (two `partial` ‚âÝ `full`; ADR-016 ¬ß2's bar on authoring
*derivable* facts stands ‚Äî degree is authored fact, not derived). The rest of ¬ß2 holds:
coverage, temporal planned-vs-done, and altitude remain projections.

**4. A value-burndown priority post-pass.** `Slices` is removed from both `REF_LABELS` and
`CONSEQUENCE_LABELS`; `Fulfils` joins **`REF_LABELS` only**. A new post-pass **subtracts** a
backlog item's value by the raw `value` facet of the slices fulfilling it (value-denominated;
gated on the delivering slice-status set `{started, audit, reconcile, done}` ‚Äî pre-work
statuses burn 0). The old `slices` ‚Üí additive-optionality credit is **dropped, not replaced**
(proven false by the forcing fact above). Degree is **out of scoring** entirely (it feeds
inbound display and the deferred close-cascade hint, IMP-210). Burndown is **non-conserving**
across multi-item fulfilment ‚Äî a slice fulfilling A and B burns its value from each
(leverage-like, deliberate).

> **ADR-016's deciding principle upheld.** Separate LABEL when the distinction changes
> structure, graph-effect, or inbound semantics; ROLE when it only refines intent. `fulfils`
> earns a new **label** (distinct inbound "fulfilled by"; distinct graph-effect ‚Äî burndown,
> not optionality). `originates_from` is a **role** ‚Äî same `references` structure, refined
> intent. Edge identity is `(label, role, target)`; degree is excluded from identity.

## Consequences

### Positive

- `slices` is retired; the three dimensions it conflated ‚Äî provenance, fulfilment,
  completion ‚Äî are each expressible at their own seam. IMP-207 (the 19-row `spawned_from`
  retcon) and IMP-149 (the `slices` ambiguity) are discharged; RFC-003's Axis C completion
  hole is closed.
- The vocabulary stays auditable: adding a degree value or role is a closed-enum code
  change, not a free-text value.

### Negative

- Degree is hand-authored with no derivation path; it is changed via `unlink`+`relink`, not
  upsert (no mutation path in `append_relation_row`).
- Value-burndown is non-conserving ‚Äî a slice fulfilling several items burns its value from
  each. This is intended leverage but can surprise a reader expecting conservation.
- The `fulfils(full)` close-cascade consumer is **not** built here (IMP-210); a default
  value floor for valueless entities is a separate sibling slice (burndown only governs
  explicitly-valued items until then).

### Neutral

- `drift` is untouched ‚Äî the free-text/non-entity escape hatch stays deferred (IMP-012,
  IDE-015). Two bare-entity drift rows (ISS-041‚ÜíRFC-003, ISS-048‚ÜíIMP-148) fit neither
  migration outlet and are consciously left under RFC-003 OQ-2 (Class 6, VH-1 approved
  2026-06-29); the decision-of-record lives in `migration-dispositions.{md,toml}`.
- Inbound rendering gains a degree dimension; cordage overlay keying stays label-keyed.
