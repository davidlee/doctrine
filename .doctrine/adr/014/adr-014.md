# ADR-014: RFC: governance-neutral first-class kind, precursor to Revision

## Context

Doctrine's kind taxonomy had no home for *deliberation* — the "think in public,
decide nothing yet" artifact that precedes a decision. Discussion either hid
inside a slice's design prose (lost once the slice closed) or was forced into a
governance kind (ADR/POL/STD) that asserts canon it does not yet hold. Two
distinct gaps follow, and they are independently contestable:

1. **The taxonomy lacks an outcome-neutral durable slot.** Every existing
   authored kind either decides something (ADR/POL/STD), specifies something
   (SPEC/PRD/REQ), or tracks work (SL/REC/REV). None offers a citable home for an
   open question that asserts nothing.
2. **The change axis has no precursor interface.** ADR-013 made the Revision
   (`REV`) the standalone change-axis anchor through which governance/spec change
   is staged and approved. But a Revision often *originates in* a prior
   deliberation, and ADR-013 modelled no edge for that provenance. Recording it
   needs a typed, outcome-neutral edge that does not bias the Revision toward any
   verdict.

Both gaps are settled by SL-122. This ADR records the two contestable claims;
the lifecycle states (§2), directory naming (§3), and RFC's `AnyNumbered`
participation in its own `related` edges (which rides ADR-004/ADR-010) stay
design-level and are not re-decided here.

## Decision

**D1 — RFC is a governance-neutral, first-class authored kind.** RFC (`RFC-NNN`)
joins the kind taxonomy as a durable, citable artifact for deliberation that
**asserts no canon**. It sources no ADR/POL/STD governance edge, carries no
in-force/required semantics, and is **structurally absent from every governance
surface** — it does not appear in the boot snapshot's Accepted ADRs / Active
Policies / Standards sections, and it confers no authority. Its status machine
(`open → resolved | withdrawn`) is outcome-neutral: resolution records that the
discussion concluded, never *what* was decided. The taxonomy gains a "think in
public, decide nothing" slot that was previously missing.

**D2 — RFC is the precursor on the change axis, amending ADR-013.** A Revision
may originate in a prior RFC. We record that provenance as a single
`originates_from` edge **REV → RFC**: typed (`Tier::Typed`), **revision-owned**
(`LinkPolicy::TypedVerbOnly` — authored only by `doctrine revision new
--originates-from RFC-NNN`, never by generic `doctrine link`), and
**outcome-neutral** (no yes-bias, no coupling to the Revision's status or
approval). The reciprocal renders on the RFC as the pure-derived inbound
"precursor of: REV-NNN" — no reverse edge is stored on the RFC (ADR-004).

This edge **adds a new outbound interface to ADR-013's change-axis model**. This
ADR therefore **explicitly amends ADR-013** on the REV precursor interface: it
extends ADR-013's Revision with the `originates_from` edge, stated here in prose
and cross-linked by a governance `related` edge (ADR → ADR, SameKind). It is
**not** a `supersedes` — ADR-013 stays **accepted**; the amendment extends it,
does not retire it.

## Consequences

### Positive
- Deliberation gets a durable, citable home that survives slice closure and does
  not masquerade as decided canon.
- The change axis gains an honest provenance trail: a Revision can name the
  discussion it grew from, without that discussion claiming authority.
- The edge is outcome-neutral and revision-owned, so it cannot be abused to
  imply a verdict or to bypass REV authoring discipline.

### Negative
- One more kind to learn and maintain; a new `originates_from` rule row and
  `RelationLabel` variant widen the relation vocabulary.

### Neutral
- RFC participation in its own `related` edges and its lifecycle states are
  design-level, not governed by this ADR — they ride existing ADRs (ADR-004,
  ADR-010) and the slice design.
- The amendment to ADR-013 is additive: existing ADR-013 behaviour is unchanged;
  only a new outbound REV edge is introduced.

## Verification

- D1 is checked by RFC's structural absence from governance projection: the boot
  snapshot's governance sections (Accepted ADRs / Active Policies / Standards)
  carry no RFC entry, and `doctrine status` surfaces RFC as work-section
  awareness only, never as governance (SL-122 PHASE-04 VT-3 boot byte-unchanged).
- D2 is checked by the relation rules and CLI: `originates_from` is `Tier::Typed`
  + `TypedVerbOnly` (generic `doctrine link … originates_from …` is refused);
  `doctrine revision new --originates-from RFC-NNN` authors the edge; `inspect
  RFC-NNN` renders the derived "precursor of: REV-NNN" with no stored reverse edge
  (SL-122 PHASE-03 VT-1..VT-4).
