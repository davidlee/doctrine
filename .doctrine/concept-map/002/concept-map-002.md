# Concept Map: Workflow

Models the core doctrine workflow at conceptual altitude for a new user — the
mental models and vocabulary needed to use doctrine effectively. It does not
detail CLI command shapes or dispatch internals; it relates doctrine's entities
and common verbs to the jobs-to-be-done and the outcomes they produce.

## How to read it

The map has six mental models, arranged as clusters:

1. **Entry — route before you act.** Substantive work is gated by the *Routing
   Gate* (`/route`), which chooses the governing skill before any file is touched
   or command run. Its authority is the *Boot Snapshot* (regenerated by
   `doctrine boot`), and it leans on *Memory* (retrieve instead of assume). The
   gate enforces *No Code Without Approved Plan* — the load-bearing rule the
   whole lifecycle exists to uphold.

2. **Origins — where work comes from.** Three origin paths: direct *User Intent*
   becomes a *Slice*; an *Idea or Problem* is captured as a *Backlog Item* in the
   *Backlog* and promoted to a slice when scoped; external issues arrive from
   GitHub. Separately, the *Dependency Graph* drives *Survey*, which prioritises
   the *Actionable Entity* (eligible and unblocked) — doctrine tells you what to
   work on next, and that re-enters the routing gate.

3. **The slice lifecycle — an explicit state machine.** `Proposed → Design →
   Plan → Ready → Started → Audit → Reconcile → Done`, with `Design` stress-tested
   by *Inquisition* before it locks, and `Design` producing the slice's
   *Design Document*. Back-edges are predicate-driven: an *Audit Finding* may
   return the slice to `Started`; `Reconcile` may return to `Audit` or escalate to
   `Design` when the spec/governance *model itself* is inadequate. A slice may be
   abandoned. Inside `Started`, a *Phase* is built through the *TDD Loop*.

4. **Human gates.** The two load-bearing human gates (ADR-009) are `Ready`
   (approved plan — the "no code without approved plan" handoff) and `Reconcile`
   (closure). `Done` is *reached from* reconcile, not a gate itself — this
   corrects a common misreading.

5. **Specifications & the truth model — the doctrine differentiator.** Specs
   (*PRD*, *Tech Spec*, *Requirement*) own **normative truth**; *Coverage* and
   *Audit Findings* record **observed truth**. When they diverge, that is *Drift*,
   reconciled **explicitly by Reconcile — never by precedence** (the split from
   spec-driver). *Reconcile* is the sole author of *Reconciled Status*. A slice is
   governed by its specs; specs are durable truth reconciled after the fact, not
   edited by precedence.

6. **Governance & change axes.** *Slices change code*; *Revisions change specs*
   (the governance change-axis, ADR-013); an *Architectural Decision* is recorded
   as an *ADR*, which governs slices. Slice-scoped decisions live in the design
   document; project-global ones live in ADRs.

Plus three supporting clusters:

- **Membership — each kind answers a different question.** *Backlog Item* → what
  to do? · *ADR* → what was decided? · *Knowledge Record* → what do we know? ·
  *Memory* → what to retrieve? This is the intake test: work, decision, durable
  fact, or scoped knowledge — pick the right kind, don't conflate them. (Memory is
  scoped knowledge you retrieve; Knowledge Records are authored durable facts —
  distinct kinds, not the same thing.)

- **Mid-flight support.** *Consult* resolves a *Tradeoff* (don't improvise past
  an obstacle); *Record Memory* enriches *Memory*; *Handover* transfers *Context*
  to a fresh agent. These layer on the routed stage at any point in the lifecycle.

- **External interaction.** *GitHub Issues* feed the backlog; a *GitHub PR* is
  reviewed by a *Peer*; a *Team* coordinates *Dispatch*. Peers and teams are
  conduct roles on the lifecycle, not separate states.

## Execution postures

During `Started`, work is conducted as *Solo* (serial, single agent) or
*Dispatch* (parallel); *Dispatch* isolates workers via *Worktree Forks*. The map
intentionally stops there — dispatch internals (workers, deltas, integration
topology) are out of scope for this high-level view.

## What was refined from the earlier draft

- "Router" → *Routing Gate* (the `/route` skill/concept, not a component).
- Corrected the human gates: `Ready` and `Reconcile`, not `Done` (per ADR-009).
- Added the **truth model** — the core differentiator (normative vs observed,
  drift, reconcile-never-by-precedence, reconciled status) — entirely missing
  before.
- Added back-edges and the `Reconcile → Design` model-gap escalation; the
  structural closure seam (`audit → reconcile → done`) is no longer flattened
  into uniform "advances to".
- Added *Coverage*, *Revision*, the dependency-driven *Survey/Actionable*
  prioritisation, and the membership test (do/decide/know/retrieve).
- Deconflated *Memory* (scoped, retrieve) from *Knowledge Record* (authored
  durable fact).
- Trimmed dispatch internals per scope.
